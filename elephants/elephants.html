"For the first time, scientists have successfully used satellite cameras coupled with deep learning to count animals in complex geographical landscapes," said the University of Bath in a statement Tuesday. Using Maxar satellites may provide an alternate way to monitor vulnerable and enagered animals, human monitoring is often time and money intensive. What's really ingenius is the way the algorithm is able to detect these elephants and distinguish them from a variety of different landscapes, including dense woodlands. Accurate monitoring of these elephants is vital to encouraging regrowth of the population, and key to giving them protection from poachers.
